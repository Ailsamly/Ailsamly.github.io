Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

Due to the growing number of architecturally complex buildings built in recent decades, mobile building inspection robot systems are required to operate in increasingly complex environments. This leads to higher requirements for their hardware design. Existing literature on the design of building inspection robots has implicitly mentioned the impact of building environments and target defects in determining robot hardware design requirements. However, the explicit representation of what information is required to define a specific hardware requirement is still missing. To fill this gap, this paper presents an ontology that provides a high-level overview of the building and inspection domain objects that can affect the determination of robot hardware design requirements (Onto-Design). It also explores the relationship between specific mobile robot hardware requirements and features of complex buildings and their defects. The Onto-Design ontology integrates two main domain ontology models including a Robot System Model and a Building and Defect Model. A content evaluation and an automated consistency checking are conducted for the internal evaluation of the ontology. Additionally, the proposed ontology is implemented in two wall-climbing inspection robot design cases to check if the investigation of the robot’s application environment is comprehensive. The results of the validation also demonstrate that the proposed ontological model allows for a more efficient retrieval of information required for determining a particular hardware requirement.
